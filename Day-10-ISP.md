## Explanation
An Internet Service Provider (ISP) is a company that provides internet access to users and organizations. It acts as a bridge between local networks and the global internet.

ISPs offer different connection types such as fiber, broadband, mobile data, and satellite. They also manage IP addressing, routing, and bandwidth allocation.

ISPs monitor network traffic, ensure service reliability, and apply security measures to protect their infrastructure and users.

## Why This Matters in Cybersecurity
ISPs play a critical role in traffic monitoring and threat detection.  
Many cyber investigations depend on ISP logs and routing data.  
ISP security failures can impact millions of users.

## My Key Takeaway
ISPs are the backbone of internet connectivity and network security visibility.
